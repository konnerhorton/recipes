# Karachi Style Chicken Tikka

**Source:** Karachi street-food classic  
**Servings:** 4  
**Time:** 5 hours (including marination)  

**Description:** Smoky, spice-crusted leg quarters with skin on — adapted for grill or oven.

## Ingredients

- 4 leg quarters of chicken
- 1 tbsp salt
- 200 g plain yogurt
- 3 tbsp lemon juice
- 6 garlic cloves, grated
- 1 tbsp ginger, grated
- 1.5 tbsp Kashmiri red chili powder
- 1 tsp cumin powder
- 1 tsp coriander powder
- 1 tsp garam masala
- 0.5 tsp turmeric
- 0.5 tsp black pepper, coarsely ground
- 2 tbsp mustard oil
- 1 tbsp raw papaya paste (or 2 tbsp pineapple juice or half a small kiwi, mashed)

## Instructions

### Score & Salt the Chicken
1. Make 3-4 deep slashes through the skin and into the meat on each chicken leg quarter going down to the bone.
2. Rub salt into the cuts and all over the surface.

### Make the Marinade
3. In a large bowl, combine yogurt, lemon juice, garlic, ginger, Kashmiri red chili powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, garam masala, turmeric, black pepper, mustard oil, and raw papaya paste. Mix until smooth.

### Marinate the Chicken
4. Add the scored leg quarters to the marinade and coat thoroughly, pressing the paste into every slash.
5. Cover and refrigerate for 4 hours up to overnight.
6. Remove from the fridge 30 minutes before cooking so the chicken comes to room temperature.

### Method 1: Grilling
7. Set the grill on high and let it get up to heat.
8. Place the leg quarters skin-side down and grill until skin is charred and crisp, about 7 minutes. Watch for flare-ups.
9. Reduce flame to low/medium and flip chicken to non-skin side. Close the lid and cook until internal temperature at the thickest part of the thigh reaches 74°C / 165°F, roughly 25 minutes more.

### Method 2: Oven Roasting
10. Preheat oven to 220°C / 425°F. Place marinated leg quarters skin-side up on a wire rack set over a sheet pan.
11. Bake for 40 minutes until internal temp hits 74°C / 165°F.
12. Switch to broil on high for 5 minutes to blister and crisp the skin. Watch closely to avoid burning.

### Rest & Serve
13. Transfer to a cutting board and rest for 5 minutes.
14. Sprinkle generously with chaat masala, squeeze fresh lemon wedges over the top, and serve on a bed of sliced onion rings with fresh green chilies on the side. A warm naan and raita complete the plate.
